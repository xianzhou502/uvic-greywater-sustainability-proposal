# WaterWise Sustainable Marketing Proposal

## Overview

This project presents **WaterWise**, a sustainable marketing and business proposal for a greywater collection service designed to help businesses reduce freshwater consumption, lower utility costs, and improve their environmental performance.

The proposed service focuses on retrofitting older businesses with greywater collection systems. These systems collect water from sources such as sink drains and rainwater, filter the collected water, and reuse it for non-potable purposes such as toilet flushing.

This project was completed for a business minor course and demonstrates sustainable business thinking, market research, SDG alignment, implementation planning, and stakeholder-focused communication.

## Project Concept

WaterWise is designed as a practical sustainability solution for businesses that want to reduce water waste and strengthen their environmental responsibility.

The service aims to:

* Reduce freshwater use in business facilities
* Reuse water that would otherwise enter the sewer system
* Lower long-term utility costs
* Support sustainability goals
* Help businesses turn environmental claims into measurable action
* Promote responsible resource management within local communities

## Problem Statement

Freshwater is a limited and valuable resource. Many businesses use potable water for functions that do not require drinking-quality water, such as flushing toilets or supporting irrigation.

At the same time, businesses are under increasing pressure to demonstrate sustainable operations, reduce environmental impact, and align their practices with consumer expectations and institutional sustainability goals.

WaterWise addresses this issue by offering a greywater reuse system that supports both environmental responsibility and business value.

## Market Opportunity

The project identifies businesses with sustainability awareness as the main target market.

Ideal customers include businesses and institutions that:

* Already promote sustainability or eco-friendly values
* Want to reduce utility costs
* Need practical ways to improve environmental performance
* Serve customers who value sustainable business practices
* Want to strengthen brand reputation through visible sustainability action

The proposal argues that businesses are more likely to adopt WaterWise if the service helps them reduce costs, improve reputation, and demonstrate real environmental commitment.

## Target Client: University of Victoria

The project uses the University of Victoria as a key focus for implementation.

UVic is a strong potential client because:

* It has existing sustainability commitments
* It has already invested in campus sustainability initiatives
* It values environmental responsibility
* It has a large campus community
* It can benefit from reduced freshwater consumption
* It can use WaterWise to support its broader sustainability and climate action goals

The proposal recommends positioning WaterWise as a solution aligned with UVic’s values, campus operations, community impact, and long-term sustainability strategy.

## Implementation Strategy

The WaterWise implementation plan focuses on awareness, education, and stakeholder engagement.

The proposed strategy includes:

1. **Market research**
   Identify businesses and institutions with sustainability goals, environmental branding, or high water usage.

2. **Promotional materials**
   Use flyers, emails, online marketing, and short promotional videos to explain the WaterWise concept.

3. **Website development**
   Create a clear and accessible website that explains how the greywater system works, what benefits it offers, and why businesses should adopt it.

4. **Stakeholder education**
   Communicate the financial, environmental, and reputational benefits of greywater reuse.

5. **Consultation process**
   Once established, use professional consultants to meet with businesses, explain installation opportunities, and develop partnerships.

6. **Pilot implementation**
   Begin with a high-alignment institution such as UVic, then use the project as a case study for future clients.

## Limitations

This project is a conceptual business proposal rather than an implemented pilot project.

Several areas would require further development before practical launch:

* Installation cost estimation
* Technical feasibility testing
* Building-specific plumbing assessment
* Regulatory and compliance review
* Maintenance planning
* Customer willingness-to-pay research
* Financial return-on-investment modelling
* Pilot testing with a real business or institution

## Future Improvements

This project could be strengthened by:

* Creating a full business model canvas
* Adding a cost-benefit analysis
* Building a customer segmentation framework
* Designing a pilot proposal for UVic
* Estimating water savings across different building types
* Developing a marketing campaign mock-up
* Adding stakeholder interview data
* Comparing greywater reuse policies across municipalities

## Conclusion

WaterWise presents a practical sustainable marketing proposal that connects environmental responsibility with business value. By helping businesses reuse greywater for non-potable purposes, the service supports freshwater conservation, cost reduction, and stronger sustainability positioning.

The project demonstrates how sustainable marketing can move beyond promotion and become a strategy for real operational change. It also shows how business solutions can align with environmental goals, community needs, and long-term resource responsibility.
